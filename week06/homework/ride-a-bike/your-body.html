Your Body

A weekly bike ride comes with a wide variety of physical health benefits. It is scientifically proven that cycling could delay aging by 10-15 years, and on average, you can lose 13 pounds the first year of commuting.

About 300 calories are burnt every hour of bicycling, but weight loss isnâ€™t the only physical health benefit.

Cardiovascular

The heart is the most important organ and can be damaged by too much inactivity. By raising the heart rate, cycling works to increase cardiovascular fitness by 3-7%. This can reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease by 50%.

Muscle Tone

One week of no physical activity can reduce the strength of your muscular system by 50% and has harmful, long-term effects. Humans have hundreds of muscles and riding a bike works all the muscles and builds strength in every part of the body, especially the legs, thighs and hips.

Therapeutic

Cycling is therapeutic for the mind and is fun to do, which can make you happier. This is due to the exercise that controls your hormones that fight anxiety, depression and psychological problems.

Blood Pressure

Moderate cycling is known to reduce blood pressure, lowering and even preventing the chance of having a stroke and can prevent damage to organs.
