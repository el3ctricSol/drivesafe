Your Wallet

There are a variety of different ways that commuting with your bike to work will leave you with more money to spend at the end of the month. It is estimated by the Leage of American Bicyclists that it only costs about 120 dollars a year to operate a bicycle.

Cheaper Parking

Parking can cost around 10 dollars a day, but depends on where you work, so riding a bike to work once a week could save 40 dollars a month, just in parking. This does not include parking tickets that you could not get on a bike.

Insurance and Registration

The average cost for registration/insurance for a motor vehicle, accoring to AAA, is over 1500 dollars a year, while bicycle regestration is not available in most states. In the states it is available, it is only under 10 dollars a year.

Gym Expenses

Bicycle riders have far lower rates in obesity than drivers, and bicycling to work could keep you as healthy as a 200-400 dollar a year health club/gym membership.

Fuel Costs

AAA statistics show that the average car driven 15,000 miles costs over 1,900 dollars a year to fill up with gas. The only fuel bikes run on is your body fat. You can save up to 40 to 50 dollars in gas a month by with a bicycle once a week to work.
